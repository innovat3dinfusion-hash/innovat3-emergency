01
EYES OPEN — POV CONSCIOUSNESS
POV Close Up · 0:00–0:08 · Live Action
Visual Direction
TOTAL DARKNESS. Slow, blurry light bleeds in. Eyes open halfway. Blinking. Blurry concrete. Blue sky. Distant sirens. Camera SLOWLY FOCUSES — a medic's face fills the frame, leaning in with concern. Everything is muffled. Heart monitor beep begins faintly.
Script / Audio
[SFX: Muffled city noise → ringing → clearing]

[SFX: Slow heartbeat — getting stronger]

MEDIC (leaning close, urgent but calm):
"Hey — hey, can you hear me? Stay with me. What's your name? Can you tell me your name?"

[Camera blinks. POV is disoriented. The face of the medic fills the screen.]
Director Note: Shoot this with a wide-angle lens close to the ground, actor looking directly down into camera. Use slow-motion blink effect (3x blink sequence) with lens blur-to-focus rack. This is the most powerful 8 seconds of the film — take your time with it.
02
THE PROBLEM — NO ID, NO PHONE
Wide Shot Close Up · 0:08–0:20 · Live Action
Visual Direction
Wide shot of accident scene. Medic kneels beside the person. She reaches for their pocket — EMPTY. She checks nearby ground — No phone. No wallet. Quick cuts: - Skid marks on road - Scattered belongings — gone - Other medic shakes head - The medic looks helpless
Script / Audio
MEDIC 2 (to colleague):
"No ID, no phone. Nothing. We don't know who this is."

MEDIC 1 (checking vitals, frustrated):
"I need to know their blood type. Medical conditions. Anything. We're flying blind here."

[SFX: Ambulance radio crackle]
Director Note: This scene establishes the problem viscerally. The phone and wallet being stolen is implied — show the empty hands and empty pockets. No need to show the theft itself. The medic's helplessness is the emotional core.

🎬 Product Shot Instructions
Use the actual red/teal innovat3 keyring tag from the product photo. Film on a dark matte surface with a single warm spotlight from above. The tag should fill the frame edge to edge. Slow rotation 360°. Add a subtle NFC ripple animation in post. This is your hero product shot — it must look premium.

Audio Direction
Music & Sound Design
Scene 1–4 · Emergency Sequence

Start with near-silence and muffled ambient sound. Introduce a slow, low heartbeat. As the medic discovers the tag, a single clean piano note. When the card loads — a warm digital chime (like a gentle notification). The emotional tone should be tense → relief.

Scene 5–8 · Turtle & Explainer Sequence

Warm, slightly cinematic background music. South African-influenced rhythm — not heavy, just present. The turtle's voice should be recorded by a real South African voice artist, male, 30s–40s, warm and authoritative. Not too corporate. Not too casual. Think: trusted older brother energy.
